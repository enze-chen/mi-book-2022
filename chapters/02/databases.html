What materials-related databases are publicly accessible?Â¶

Here is a list of some common materials databases (and data infrastructure, more broadly) that you might consult for self-directed research:

  • AFLOW: First-principles calculated properties mostly for inorganic crystal structures.

  • Crystallography Open Database: Crystal structures of organic, inorganic, and metal-organic compounds.

  • Materials Data Facility (MDF): Data publication platform for materials datasets.

  • Open Quantum Materials Database (OQMD): First-principles calculated properties mostly for inorganic crystal structures.

  • Materials Project (MP): First-principles calculated properties similar to AFLOW and OQMD.

  • Citrination: A database of various materials data contributed by the MI community.

  • MatWeb: A database for properties of various engineering materials and their datasheets.

  • These arenâ€™t databases per se, but there are other websites with lots of materials-related data such as Wikipedia, WolframAlpha, etc.

For more sources, you can try searching on Google, reading MI review papers such as the one by Himanen et al. Adv. Sci., 2019 [1], or looking at the â€œData availabilityâ€ sections at the end of relevant papers.
